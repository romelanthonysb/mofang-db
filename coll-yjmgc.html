About this collection

I started to realize that there was a "historical" component to my cube collecting, i.e., that cubes were innovating on performance and cube feels at a rapid pace that I could observe. This led me to try to collect all the cubes of a certain model lineage (all the cubes that shared the same name). Initially I was planning to collect all the cubes called RS3 M; my reasoning was that the RS3 M 2020 was a game-changer in the way it brought pro-level features (like dual adjustment, responsiveness to lube setup, and forgiving corner performance) to a cube that was solidly on the budget tier (less than 10 USD when it came out).

However, at just that moment, MoYu decided to go on a cube design spree, releasing cube after cube seemingly every week, with each one coming out in multiple versions (maglev, core magnets, UV-coated, etc.). Moreover, the more I investigated the lineage of the RS3 M name, the more complicated it became. MoYu started out with a cube called the MF3, followed by the MF3 RS, then MF3 RS2, then the MF3 RS3, then dropping the "MF3" to get the RS3 M, then revising that cube to get the RS3 M 2020. Some of these cubes seem to not be available anymore.

I also briefly considered collecting the DaYan Guhong, another model name with a good reputation, and the X-man Design Tornado, which as of this writing is planning to come out with version 5. And indeed, it may not be too late to collect these others down the line. But I finally settled on the YJ MGC line because of the way cube reviewers always seemed to clown on this model. The wisdom of the group seems to be that YJ MGCs are good in different sizes (and the odd YouCuber still seem to recommend the MGC for larger cubes such as 6×6 and 7×7) except for the 3×3.

So, to me, there's a story there. Why can't YJ seem to get it together? What design decisions did they take that led to this uneven reputation for this brand? And are the YouCubers right? It turns out that it's quite more interesting than it seems on the surface. Many of the cubes are quite good, and the v2 and the Beta stand out for me as being very satisfying to turn. Others, while being not as performant, are interesting in their own ways. Please click through the cube displays here to read about my own findings.

Some notes

  • The only cube in this series that I have not been able to procure is the YJ MGC Repulsion. I am not certain whether this is the first mass-produced cube to use magnets that repel from the core, but that would make it really collectible. Alas, relatively few people seem to have got it, and it eventually was discontinued before I had the thought to collect it. I have placed this cube in my wishlist in case someone passing by has a lead on trading for or selling one. (Incidentally, YJ's showcase webpage does not list the Repulsion as one of their cubes, even though all the other ones (like the Evo) are included.)
  • I did hear recently about the YJ MGC Sigma, which is a cube designed by personnel at TheCubicle.com. It is an MGC Beta with modifications selected by the Cubicle team. I have not yet decided if I will collect that cube, as it was not entirely designed by YJ, but it's relatively inexpensive, and is probably not going to be discontinued as long as the Cubicle is around.
  • I also chose to exclude the YJ Meta even though it seems to be an iteration on the Beta, because its name does not include "MGC".
  • As of this writing I have almost all the cubes in my possession; however, a few are still not unboxed and described on the Instagram, owing to the busy-ness of life. Perhaps by summer this collection will be complete online.
