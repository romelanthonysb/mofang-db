About this collection

When I got the QYtoys Warrior W Jelly, I was struck at how different it looked from other cubes. It reminded some people of candy, but it reminded me of sea glass, the broken bits of glass on beaches smoothed over by sand and crashing waves. I realized that if translucent cubes existed, then transparent or clear cubes must also exist.

And exist they did, as I found out during my research. But references to clear cubes were always either in the deep past (before 2018, in terms of cube history), or as special editions that are too expensive for me to collect. This is the case for GAN's annual flagship limited edition, for example.

Apparently, clear plastic is more brittle than primary plastic, and this makes them rarer overall. While I have not been able to re-locate the source of this claim or fact, it explains why clear cubes are either old or expensive.

What's more interesting is that the many cubes in my collection are clear in different ways. Some are translucent, some have dark plastic, some have no differentiation in color and are always solved, and some are "see-through" in different ways.

If I may say so myself, even if there are fewer cubes in this collection than in my historical cubes series, these to me are clearly (get it?) the jewels of my collection. I invite you to peruse them above.

Some notes

  • To me the best-looking cube in this collection is the YJ Yulong Transparent. Because it is an older cube, it was a minor miracle that I was even able to source one. It has the best claim to the name "clear cube". I wish I could source another one so I could install magnets inside.
  • The clear cube with the best feeling in my collection is the GAN 356 M e UV, 10th Anniversary Edition. It has the makings of a modern(-ish) cube that has transparent faces.
  • This collection will probably have a low number of cubes, but I expect to collect the odd cube that catches my eye here and there.
