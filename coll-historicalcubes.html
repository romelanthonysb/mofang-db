About this collection

The reason I have a cube collection at all goes back to my choice of cubes during my first and second purchases. My first was an RS3 M 2021 Maglev, and my second purchase consisted of the GAN Speed Cube and the Cyclone Boys Metallic M.

I think that if I had not bought the metallic cube, my collection would have just been the first two. However, the Cyclone Boys Metallic had such a satisfying, clunky feeling brought about by the magnets and the lightness of the plastic that it caused me to look for that feeling in a regularly-colored speedcube.

As I watched YouCubers describe the different cubes they were influencing us to buy, they used an intriguing yet relatively opaque vocabulary (with words such as "bubbly", "papery", and others to describe cube turning feelings) and a whole host of technological innovations that affected their performance and feeling. I became curious about experiencing these different "cube feels" for myself. This was my gateway into the hobby.

Even now, years hence from September 2022, during my first purchase, I still consider myself a novice speed-solver, where I can only break the 1-minute barrier on a good day. But over time, I have grown to appreciate the different experiences the different cubes provide. And I still marvel at how far the technology has come since the DaYan GuHong, which is said to be the first proper speedcube.

Flagships/jewels of this collection

  • DaYan GuHong—considered to be the world's first "speedcube" (a cube whose design is geared toward fast turning)
  • MoYu Aolong v2—the cube designed in response to the "DNF heard around the world" (Feliks Zemdegs' 5.33 DNF in 2014)
  • GAN 356 Air UM—the first cube to have factory-installed magnets (previously, you had to mod your cube to have magnets in them, and cube stores used to offer a service to install them for you, an innovation by Chris Tran)
  • MoYu RS3 M 2020—a cube that warped the format by offering pro features like dual customization in a budget price (less than 9 USD on release)

To be honest, I can probably find something to say about each cube in this series. This is where I am most indebted to my list of sources, who took the time to briefly describe cubes all those years ago.
